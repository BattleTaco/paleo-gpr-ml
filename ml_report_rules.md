For ML engineering reports and summaries:

- Write as a practitioner, not a textbook.
- Focus on the actual problem, the data, the modeling choice, and what happened.
- Mention tradeoffs naturally.
- Avoid generic statements about AI or machine learning.
- If results are weak or mixed, say so plainly and explain why that matters.
- Prefer grounded observations like:
  - performance changed after normalization
  - likely sensitivity to class imbalance
  - feature leakage risk
  - limited generalization due to dataset scope
- Do not oversell results.
- Make the writing sound like someone who did the work and reflected on it honestly.
